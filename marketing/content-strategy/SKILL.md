# Content Strategy Skill

A systematic approach to planning, creating, and distributing content that attracts the right audience, builds trust, and drives measurable business outcomes. Great content strategy aligns every piece of content with audience needs and business goals — turning content from a cost center into a growth engine.

## Core Principle

**Create content your audience searches for, not content your company wants to say.** The best content answers real questions, solves real problems, and earns attention by being genuinely useful — not by broadcasting brand messages.

## Scoring

**Goal: 10/10.** When reviewing or creating content strategy, rate 0–10 based on audience alignment, clarity of goals, consistency of execution, and measurable results. A 10/10 means every content piece has a clear purpose, target audience, and success metric.

## Strategy Framework

### 1. Audience Research

**Core concept:** Deep understanding of who your audience is, what they care about, and where they spend their attention.

**Why it matters:** Content created without audience insight is guesswork. Audience research is the foundation of everything.

**Key practices:**
- Build 2–3 detailed audience personas (role, pain points, goals, content preferences)
- Interview existing customers about their challenges and information sources
- Audit competitor content to identify gaps and opportunities
- Use search data (Google Search Console, keyword tools) to find what your audience is actively searching for
- Monitor where your audience spends time online (LinkedIn, industry forums, newsletters)

**Persona template:**

| Field | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Name | "Operations Manager Omar" |
| Role | Operations Manager, mid-sized hospitality group |
| Pain points | Staff scheduling chaos, inconsistent guest experience across properties |
| Goals | Streamline operations, reduce cost per available room |
| Content preference | Short practical guides, checklists, case studies — not theory |
| Where they read | LinkedIn, industry association newsletters, Google search |

See: [references/audience-personas.md](references/audience-personas.md)

### 2. Content Goals & Metrics

**Core concept:** Every content initiative has a clear business goal tied to a measurable metric.

**Why it matters:** Without goals, content devolves into output for its own sake.

**Goal → Metric mapping:**

| Business Goal | Content Goal | Key Metric |
|--------------|-------------|------------|
| Brand awareness | Reach new audiences | Impressions, new visitors |
| Lead generation | Capture contact details | Leads, form fills, trial signups |
| Sales enablement | Support sales conversations | Content usage by sales team, deal velocity |
| Customer retention | Educate and retain customers | Engagement rate, renewal correlation |
| SEO / organic traffic | Rank for target keywords | Organic search traffic, keyword rankings |

### 3. Content Calendar & Planning

**Core concept:** A structured editorial calendar that ensures consistent, high-quality output without last-minute scrambles.

**Why it matters:** Inconsistent publishing destroys audience trust and algorithm performance.

**Key practices:**
- Plan content 4–6 weeks ahead minimum
- Map content themes to business calendar (product launches, seasons, industry events)
- Mix content types: long-form (pillar), short-form (social), repurposed (snippets from long-form)
- Assign owners, deadlines, and distribution channels for every piece
- Build a content bank of evergreen pieces that can be published any time

**Content type mix (recommended starting point):**

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Long-form blog / guide | 2x/month | SEO, thought leadership |
| Social posts | 3–5x/week | Awareness, engagement |
| Email newsletter | 1–2x/month | Retention, nurture |
| Case study | 1x/quarter | Sales enablement, proof |
| Video / webinar | 1x/quarter | Brand authority |

### 4. Brand Voice & Style

**Core concept:** A consistent, distinctive voice that makes your content instantly recognizable as yours.

**Why it matters:** Inconsistent voice fragments brand identity and reduces trust.

**Key practices:**
- Define 3–5 voice attributes (e.g., "Direct, Warm, Expert, Practical")
- Write do/don't examples for each attribute
- Create a style guide with preferred terminology, tone for different channels, and formatting standards
- Apply the brand voice guide to all content reviews

See: [references/brand-voice-guide.md](references/brand-voice-guide.md)

### 5. Distribution & Amplification

**Core concept:** Great content without distribution reaches no one. Distribution is not an afterthought.

**Why it matters:** Most content gets 80% of its potential value from the first 48 hours of distribution.

**Distribution checklist per piece:**
- [ ] Published on owned channels (blog, website)
- [ ] Promoted on social media (organic)
- [ ] Sent to email list (if relevant)
- [ ] Shared by team members personally (LinkedIn especially)
- [ ] Repurposed into shorter formats (quote cards, threads, clips)
- [ ] Submitted to relevant communities / newsletters (guest post, link share)
- [ ] Pitched to industry publications (for pillar content)

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Creating content without audience research | Writes what the company wants to say, not what audience needs | Start with 3 customer interviews before any content planning |
| No editorial calendar | Last-minute, inconsistent publishing | Build a 6-week rolling calendar; treat deadlines like meetings |
| Measuring vanity metrics (likes, impressions) | Doesn't connect content to business outcomes | Define one business metric per content goal |
| Treating every channel the same | Different audiences need different formats | Adapt content natively for each platform |
| Never repurposing content | Wastes high-effort long-form content | Build a repurposing workflow into every pillar piece |

## Quick Diagnostic

| Question | If No | Action |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Do we have written audience personas? | Flying blind | Conduct 3 customer interviews this week |
| Does every content piece have a defined goal and metric? | No accountability | Add goal + metric to the editorial brief template |
| Is content publishing consistent (same cadence each week)? | Audience trust eroding | Reduce volume to what can be sustained consistently |
| Are we repurposing long-form into social/email? | Leaving value on the table | Build repurposing into the content workflow |
