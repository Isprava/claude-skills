# Marketing Decision Playbooks

## Playbook Format
- **Name:** Descriptive action name
- **When to Use:** Trigger conditions
- **Preconditions:** Requirements before executing
- **Steps:** Ordered action sequence
- **Expected Impact:** Measurable outcomes
- **Risks / Failure Modes:** What could go wrong
- **Owner(s):** Responsible team/role
- **Timeline:** Expected duration

---

## Playbook D1: CAC Reduction Sprint

### When to Use
- CAC increased >15% MoM
- LTV:CAC ratio dropped below 3:1
- CAC above industry benchmark by >20%

### Preconditions
- Have channel-level CAC breakdown
- Have at least 30 days of campaign data
- Conversion tracking verified accurate

### Steps
1. **Segment Analysis** (Day 1-2)
   - Break down CAC by channel, campaign, audience
   - Identify top 3 CAC contributors
   - Calculate marginal CAC for each segment

2. **Quick Wins** (Day 3-5)
   - Pause campaigns with CAC >2x average
   - Reduce bids on underperforming keywords/audiences
   - Exclude low-intent placements

3. **Efficiency Optimization** (Day 6-14)
   - Refresh creatives on top-spend campaigns
   - Test new audience segments
   - Implement/refine audience exclusions
   - Optimize landing page conversion

4. **Budget Reallocation** (Day 15-21)
   - Shift budget to lowest CAC channels
   - Test incremental spend on winning segments
   - Set CAC ceiling alerts

5. **Measurement** (Day 22-30)
   - Compare before/after CAC
   - Validate volume maintenance
   - Document learnings

### Expected Impact
- CAC reduction: 15-30%
- Timeframe: 30 days
- Volume impact: -5% to +10%

### Risks / Failure Modes
- Volume may drop if cuts too aggressive
- Channel reallocation may not scale
- Short-term focus may hurt long-term building

### Owner(s)
- Performance Marketing Manager
- Growth Marketing Lead

### Timeline
- 30 days active execution
- 2-week stabilization period

## Playbook D2: Channel Diversification

### When to Use
- Single channel >60% of conversions
- Primary channel efficiency declining
- Platform policy/algorithm changes announced
- Need to scale beyond current channel ceiling

### Preconditions
- Understand current channel attribution model
- Have budget available for testing
- Creative assets adaptable to new channels

### Steps
1. **Channel Assessment** (Week 1)
   - Audit current channel mix and performance
   - Identify 2-3 potential new channels
   - Research channel audience overlap
   - Estimate test budgets required

2. **Test Planning** (Week 2)
   - Define success criteria for new channels
   - Create channel-specific creatives
   - Set up tracking and attribution
   - Allocate 10-15% of budget to tests

3. **Pilot Execution** (Weeks 3-6)
   - Launch campaigns on new channels
   - Run for minimum viable sample size
   - A/B test creative approaches
   - Monitor daily, optimize weekly

4. **Evaluation** (Week 7)
   - Compare new channel performance to primary
   - Calculate blended portfolio efficiency
   - Identify scaling potential

5. **Scale or Kill** (Week 8+)
   - Double down on performing channels
   - Shut down underperformers
   - Integrate winners into main strategy

### Expected Impact
- Reduce dependency: Primary channel <50% of mix
- Discover 1-2 scalable alternatives
- Improve portfolio risk profile

### Risks / Failure Modes
- Test budget may not yield conclusive results
- New channels may not scale
- Attribution may be complex across channels
- Team bandwidth stretched

### Owner(s)
- Head of Growth
- Channel Specialists

### Timeline
- 8-week pilot program
- Ongoing optimization thereafter

## Playbook D3: LTV Improvement Initiative

### When to Use
- LTV:CAC ratio <3:1 due to low LTV
- Churn rate increasing
- Repeat purchase rate declining
- Customer lifetime shortening

### Preconditions
- Have cohort data for LTV analysis
- Can track customer behavior over time
- Have access to customer feedback

### Steps
1. **Diagnostic Phase** (Week 1-2)
   - Segment LTV by acquisition source
   - Analyze retention curves by cohort
   - Review churn reasons
   - Survey churned customers

2. **Quick Retention Wins** (Week 3-4)
   - Implement churn prediction alerts
   - Launch win-back campaigns
   - Fix top customer complaints
   - Improve onboarding experience

3. **Value Optimization** (Week 5-8)
   - Develop upsell/cross-sell programs
   - Launch loyalty/rewards program
   - Implement personalization
   - Create expansion revenue paths

4. **Acquisition Quality** (Week 9-12)
   - Tighten lead qualification
   - Shift budget to high-LTV sources
   - Update ICP based on LTV data
   - Train sales on ideal customer fit

5. **Measurement** (Ongoing)
   - Track LTV by cohort
   - Monitor retention improvements
   - Calculate ROI of initiatives

### Expected Impact
- LTV increase: 20-40%
- Churn reduction: 10-25%
- LTV:CAC improvement: 0.5-1.0 points

### Risks / Failure Modes
- LTV improvements take time to materialize
- May conflict with growth targets
- Retention tactics may cannibalize new sales

### Owner(s)
- Customer Success Lead
- Product Manager
- Marketing Lead

### Timeline
- 12-week initial program
- 6-month measurement period

## Playbook D4: Funnel Conversion Recovery

### When to Use
- Stage conversion rate dropped >20%
- Funnel bottleneck identified
- Pipeline velocity slowing
- Lead-to-customer rate declining

### Preconditions
- Clear funnel stage definitions
- Tracking at each stage
- Ability to A/B test

### Steps
1. **Bottleneck Identification** (Day 1-3)
   - Map full funnel with conversion rates
   - Identify biggest drop-off point
   - Segment by source, device, time
   - Review recent changes

2. **Root Cause Analysis** (Day 4-7)
   - Technical audit (errors, speed)
   - UX review (friction points)
   - Message audit (clarity, relevance)
   - Competitive analysis

3. **Hypothesis Generation** (Day 8-10)
   - List potential fixes prioritized by impact
   - Design A/B tests
   - Define success metrics

4. **Testing Phase** (Week 2-4)
   - Implement highest-priority tests
   - Run to statistical significance
   - Monitor secondary metrics

5. **Implementation** (Week 5-6)
   - Roll out winning variations
   - Document learnings
   - Identify next optimization areas

### Expected Impact
- Stage CVR improvement: 15-50%
- Overall funnel improvement: 10-30%
- Revenue impact: 15-40%

### Risks / Failure Modes
- Tests may be inconclusive
- Improvements may shift bottleneck
- May impact other funnel stages

### Owner(s)
- Growth Marketing
- Product/UX Team
- CRO Specialist

### Timeline
- 6-week sprint
- Iterative optimization ongoing

## Playbook D5: Budget Reallocation

### When to Use
- Channel efficiency varies significantly
- New budget available or cuts required
- Quarterly/annual planning cycle
- Performance review indicates imbalance

### Preconditions
- Have reliable channel performance data
- Understand incrementality (not just last-click)
- Have flexibility in channel agreements

### Steps
1. **Performance Audit** (Day 1-3)
   - Compile all channel metrics
   - Calculate efficiency scores (ROAS, CAC, LTV)
   - Identify overperformers and underperformers
   - Assess scaling potential of each

2. **Incrementality Assessment** (Day 4-7)
   - Evaluate true incremental impact
   - Account for assisted conversions
   - Consider brand halo effects
   - Model cross-channel effects

3. **Reallocation Modeling** (Day 8-10)
   - Model 3 scenarios: conservative, moderate, aggressive
   - Predict volume and efficiency outcomes
   - Identify risks of each scenario

4. **Stakeholder Alignment** (Day 11-14)
   - Present analysis and recommendations
   - Address concerns and questions
   - Agree on reallocation plan

5. **Execution** (Day 15-30)
   - Implement changes incrementally (20% shifts weekly)
   - Monitor closely for unexpected impacts
   - Adjust based on early signals

6. **Review** (Day 31-45)
   - Compare actual vs predicted outcomes
   - Fine-tune allocations
   - Document for future planning

### Expected Impact
- Overall efficiency: +10-25%
- Same budget, better results
- More predictable outcomes

### Risks / Failure Modes
- Scaling may not work as modeled
- Channels may have minimum spend requirements
- Competitor behavior may change
- Seasonality may confuse results

### Owner(s)
- Head of Marketing
- Performance Marketing Lead
- Finance Partner

### Timeline
- 6-week planning and execution
- Monthly review thereafter

## Playbook D6: Creative Refresh Sprint

### When to Use
- CTR declining >15%
- Creative running >45 days unchanged
- Ad fatigue indicators present
- CPM rising without CTR improvement

### Preconditions
- Have creative performance data
- Have design/production resources
- Have approved brand guidelines

### Steps
1. **Audit** (Day 1-2)
   - Analyze creative performance by age
   - Identify fatigue patterns
   - Benchmark against industry CTRs

2. **Concept Development** (Day 3-5)
   - Develop 3-5 new concepts
   - Vary format, message, visual approach
   - Include video if not already testing

3. **Production** (Day 6-10)
   - Create assets for all platforms
   - Ensure brand consistency
   - Prepare variations for testing

4. **Launch & Test** (Day 11-20)
   - Roll out new creatives
   - Test against existing winners
   - Monitor performance daily

5. **Optimization** (Day 21-30)
   - Double down on winners
   - Iterate on promising concepts
   - Retire underperformers
   - Document learnings

### Expected Impact
- CTR improvement: 20-50%
- CPC reduction: 10-25%
- Renewed engagement

### Risks / Failure Modes
- New creatives may underperform
- Production may delay timeline
- Message may not resonate

### Owner(s)
- Creative Team
- Performance Marketing
- Brand Manager

### Timeline
- 30-day sprint
- Monthly refresh cadence established

## Playbook D7: Attribution Model Optimization

### When to Use
- Last-click attribution causing budget misallocation
- Upper-funnel investments questioned
- Cross-channel effects not understood
- Major strategic decisions pending

### Preconditions
- Multi-touch data available
- Technical capability to implement
- Stakeholder buy-in for change

### Steps
1. **Current State Assessment** (Week 1)
   - Document current attribution model
   - Identify known gaps/biases
   - Gather stakeholder pain points

2. **Data Preparation** (Week 2)
   - Audit data quality
   - Ensure cross-channel tracking
   - Validate conversion paths

3. **Model Evaluation** (Week 3-4)
   - Test alternative models (linear, time-decay, data-driven)
   - Compare outputs across models
   - Assess impact on budget decisions

4. **Implementation** (Week 5-6)
   - Roll out preferred model
   - Update reporting dashboards
   - Train team on interpretation

5. **Calibration** (Week 7-8)
   - Run holdout tests to validate
   - Adjust model weights if needed
   - Document methodology

### Expected Impact
- Better budget allocation
- More informed strategic decisions
- Fairer channel performance view

### Risks / Failure Modes
- Complexity may overwhelm team
- Stakeholders may resist change
- Data quality issues may persist

### Owner(s)
- Marketing Analytics Lead
- Data Engineering
- Channel Managers

### Timeline
- 8-week implementation
- Quarterly recalibration
