# Campaign Planning Skill

A structured framework for planning, executing, and measuring integrated marketing campaigns that achieve defined business outcomes. Great campaigns are built backwards from a clear goal, executed consistently across channels, and measured rigorously so every campaign makes the next one smarter.

## Core Principle

**Clarity before creativity.** Before choosing a channel, format, or message, you must know exactly who you are talking to, what you want them to do, and how you will know if it worked. Strategy first, tactics second.

## Scoring

**Goal: 10/10.** When reviewing or building a campaign plan, rate 0–10 based on goal clarity, audience specificity, channel fit, budget efficiency, and measurement rigor. A 10/10 means the campaign has a single clear goal, the right audience, the right message, the right channels, and a defined measurement framework.

## Campaign Planning Framework

### 1. Campaign Brief

**Core concept:** A one-page document that aligns everyone on what the campaign is, who it's for, and what success looks like — before any work begins.

**Why it matters:** Campaigns without a brief drift. Teams build beautiful assets toward the wrong goal.

**Brief template:**

| Field | Guidance |
|-------|----------|
| **Campaign name** | Short, memorable internal name |
| **Business goal** | The one metric this campaign must move (e.g., 50 new leads, 20% increase in bookings) |
| **Target audience** | Specific persona + segment (not "everyone") |
| **Key message** | The single most important thing the audience should take away |
| **Offer / CTA** | What we are asking them to do (download, book, buy, register) |
| **Channels** | Where the campaign will run |
| **Budget** | Total and by channel |
| **Timeline** | Start, end, key milestones |
| **Success metrics** | 1 primary KPI + 2 supporting metrics |

### 2. Goal Setting (SMART)

**Core concept:** Every campaign has one primary business goal, expressed as a SMART target.

**Why it matters:** Vague goals produce vague results. SMART goals create accountability and enable optimization.

**SMART check:**

| Letter | Meaning | Bad example | Good example |
|--------|---------|-------------|--------------|
| S | Specific | "Increase awareness" | "Increase website traffic from LinkedIn" |
| M | Measurable | "Get more leads" | "Generate 100 qualified leads" |
| A | Achievable | "10x revenue in 30 days" | "Increase leads by 30% vs last quarter" |
| R | Relevant | "Go viral on TikTok" (B2B company) | "Increase demo requests from target accounts" |
| T | Time-bound | "Eventually improve" | "By end of Q2 2024" |

### 3. Audience Segmentation

**Core concept:** Identify the specific segment of your audience this campaign speaks to, and tailor everything to them.

**Why it matters:** A campaign that tries to speak to everyone speaks to no one.

**Segmentation dimensions:**
- **Firmographic** (B2B): Company size, industry, geography, revenue
- **Demographic** (B2C): Age, location, income, role
- **Behavioral**: Past purchases, website behavior, engagement level
- **Stage in buyer journey**: Awareness / Consideration / Decision

**Campaign audience statement template:**
> "This campaign targets [persona name] who are [behavioral qualifier, e.g., 'evaluating project management software for the first time'] and are at the [awareness/consideration/decision] stage of their journey."

### 4. Channel Strategy

**Core concept:** Choose channels based on where your target audience actually spends time, not where it's easiest to publish.

**Why it matters:** The right message on the wrong channel is wasted budget.

**Channel selection matrix:**

| Channel | Best for | Strength | Weakness |
|---------|----------|----------|----------|
| LinkedIn | B2B decision makers | Targeting precision | High CPM, low volume |
| Google Search | High-intent buyers | Captures demand | Competitive, can be expensive |
| Email | Existing contacts / nurture | High ROI, direct | Requires list; deliverability risk |
| Meta (Facebook/Instagram) | B2C, visual products | Scale, retargeting | Lower B2B intent |
| Content / SEO | Long-term organic growth | Compounds over time | Slow to start |
| Events / Sponsorship | Relationship building | High trust | High cost, hard to measure |

### 5. Message Architecture

**Core concept:** One primary message, adapted for each channel and audience segment.

**Why it matters:** Inconsistent messages confuse buyers and dilute brand impact.

**Message hierarchy:**
1. **Core message**: The single most important thing (headline)
2. **Supporting messages**: 2–3 proof points (why believe it)
3. **CTA**: What to do next (one action)

**Message testing checklist:**
- [ ] Is the core message immediately clear in under 5 seconds?
- [ ] Does it speak to the audience's pain, not the company's features?
- [ ] Is there one clear CTA, not three?
- [ ] Is the message consistent across all touchpoints?

### 6. Budget Allocation

**Core concept:** Allocate budget based on channel efficiency and campaign goal — not habit.

**Key principle:** Spend where your audience is and where you can measure ROI.

**Budget allocation guidance:**

| Campaign Goal | Budget Split (starting point) |
|--------------|-------------------------------|
| Awareness | 60% reach channels (social, display), 40% content/PR |
| Lead generation | 50% paid search, 30% paid social, 20% landing page / CRO |
| Nurture / Retention | 70% email, 20% retargeting, 10% content |

### 7. Measurement & Reporting

**Core concept:** Define measurement before launch so you know what data to capture.

**Key practices:**
- Set up UTM parameters on all campaign links before launching
- Define your primary KPI and two supporting metrics in the brief
- Review campaign performance at mid-point (optimize) and end-point (learn)
- Document learnings in a campaign debrief for future reference

**Standard campaign metrics:**

| Funnel Stage | Metric | Why It Matters |
|-------------|--------|---------------|
| Reach | Impressions, reach | Did we get in front of the right people? |
| Engagement | CTR, engagement rate | Did the message resonate? |
| Conversion | Leads, trials, sales | Did it drive the business action? |
| Efficiency | CPL, CPA, ROAS | Was the budget used effectively? |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Starting with channel ("Let's do Instagram") | Channel before strategy means wrong audience | Start with goal → audience → channel |
| Multiple competing CTAs | Confuses the audience, kills conversion | One campaign, one CTA |
| Not setting up tracking before launch | Can't measure results or optimize | UTMs + conversion tracking required before go-live |
| Running campaigns without a debrief | Same mistakes in every campaign | Mandatory 1-page debrief within 1 week of campaign end |
| Equal budget across all channels | Wastes money on underperforming channels | Allocate to highest-ROI channels; reallocate at mid-point |

## Quick Diagnostic

| Question | If No | Action |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Is there a written campaign brief? | Misalignment risk | Write the brief before any creative work begins |
| Is there one primary KPI for the campaign? | No accountability | Define the single success metric now |
| Are UTM parameters set up before launch? | Can't measure ROI | Set up UTMs and conversion tracking immediately |
| Is the target audience a specific segment? | Campaign is too broad | Narrow the audience; create separate campaigns if needed |
