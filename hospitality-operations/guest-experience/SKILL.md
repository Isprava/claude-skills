# Guest Experience Skill

A framework for consistently delivering exceptional guest experiences that build loyalty, generate positive reviews, and drive repeat business. Great hospitality is not about fancy amenities — it is about anticipating needs, recovering from failures gracefully, and making every guest feel genuinely valued.

## Core Principle

**Hospitality is an emotion, not a process.** Guests remember how you made them feel, not the thread count of the sheets. Every interaction — from booking to departure — is an opportunity to create a memory. The best operations design these moments intentionally, train their teams to deliver them consistently, and measure the results rigorously.

## Scoring

**Goal: 10/10.** When reviewing guest experience standards, rate 0–10 based on consistency of service delivery, quality of staff interactions, speed of problem resolution, and guest satisfaction metrics. A 10/10 means guests consistently report feeling genuinely welcomed, their needs are anticipated rather than just responded to, and complaints are recovered so well that the guest becomes an advocate.

## Guest Experience Framework

### 1. The Guest Journey Mapping

**Core concept:** Map every touchpoint a guest has with your property — from first awareness to post-stay — and design each one intentionally.

**Why it matters:** Inconsistent experiences across touchpoints destroy overall satisfaction. A brilliant check-in cannot rescue a terrible booking experience.

**Guest journey touchpoints:**

| Stage | Touchpoints | Experience Goal |
|-------|------------|----------------|
| **Awareness** | Online listing, reviews, social media | "This property looks exactly right for us" |
| **Booking** | Booking engine, confirmation email, pre-arrival communication | "Easy, clear, and I feel taken care of already" |
| **Pre-arrival** | Pre-arrival email, special requests follow-up | "They've noticed my preferences; I'm excited to arrive" |
| **Arrival** | Car park, entrance, check-in, room orientation | "Warm welcome; everything is ready and exactly as expected" |
| **In-stay** | Room quality, F&B, amenities, staff interactions, issue handling | "Every interaction feels genuine; my needs are met before I ask" |
| **Departure** | Check-out, farewell, luggage assistance | "They made leaving as pleasant as arriving" |
| **Post-stay** | Thank-you email, review request, loyalty follow-up | "I want to go back; I'll tell my friends" |

See: [references/guest-journey-template.md](references/guest-journey-template.md)

### 2. Service Standards

**Core concept:** Written, trained, and enforced standards for every guest interaction so that the experience is consistent regardless of which team member is working.

**Why it matters:** Inconsistency is the enemy of luxury. When a guest gets brilliant service from one team member and mediocre service from another, the mediocre experience is what they remember and review.

**Service standard elements:**

| Element | Standard | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Greeting | Within 10 seconds of guest approach | "Good morning, welcome to [Property]. How may I assist you?" |
| Name use | Use guest name minimum 3x during check-in | "Mr. Johnson, your room is..." |
| Eye contact | Maintained during all conversations | Staff do not look at screens while speaking to guests |
| Anticipation | Address needs before guest asks | Note guests approaching the bar; have menus ready |
| Complaint handling | Acknowledge, apologize, act within 5 min | See service recovery framework below |
| Farewell | All departing guests acknowledged | "Thank you for staying with us, Mr. Johnson. We hope to see you again." |

### 3. Service Recovery

**Core concept:** A systematic process for turning a guest complaint into a loyalty-building moment.

**Why it matters:** Research consistently shows that guests whose complaints are resolved excellently become more loyal than guests who had no complaint at all. Service recovery is a loyalty opportunity.

**Service recovery framework (LEARN):**

| Letter | Step | What to Do |
|--------|------|-----------|
| **L** | Listen | Give the guest your full attention. Do not interrupt. Do not be defensive. |
| **E** | Empathize | "I completely understand why that's frustrating, and I'm sorry." |
| **A** | Apologize | Genuine apology — do not make excuses |
| **R** | Resolve | Fix the problem immediately. Escalate if needed. Offer compensation proportional to the issue. |
| **N** | Notify | Follow up to confirm the issue is resolved and the guest is satisfied. Document the complaint. |

**Compensation guidelines:**

| Issue Severity | Response | Compensation Level |
|---------------|----------|--------------------|
| Minor (noisy neighbour, slow service) | Immediate fix + apology | Complimentary amenity (drink, dessert) |
| Moderate (room issue, wrong booking) | Fix + genuine apology + follow-up | Room upgrade or F&B credit |
| Serious (significant disruption to stay) | Management involvement + compensation | Night comp or meaningful F&B/spa credit |
| Critical (safety issue, major system failure) | GM involvement + full resolution | Full night comp + letter of apology |

### 4. Guest Feedback Management

**Core concept:** A systematic process to collect, analyze, act on, and respond to all forms of guest feedback.

**Why it matters:** Unread feedback is wasted insight. Unreplied reviews are missed loyalty opportunities.

**Feedback channels and response SLAs:**

| Channel | Response SLA | Owner |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| In-stay feedback (verbal) | Immediate | Duty manager |
| Post-stay survey | Read within 24h; act on patterns monthly | Guest relations |
| Online review (TripAdvisor, Google) | Reply within 48 hours | Guest relations / GM |
| Social media mention | Reply within 4 hours | Marketing / Guest relations |
| Direct complaint email | Acknowledge within 4 hours; resolve within 24h | Guest relations |

**Online review response principles:**
- Thank the reviewer by name
- Acknowledge specific comments (shows you actually read it)
- Apologize for negatives without making excuses
- Describe corrective action taken
- Invite them back
- Keep it short — responses are read by future guests too

### 5. Team Training & Standards Culture

**Core concept:** Standards only work if teams are trained, motivated, and held accountable to them.

**Why it matters:** Guest experience is ultimately delivered by people. Systems support them, but people make or break it.

**Training framework:**

| Training Type | Frequency | Content |
|--------------|-----------|---------|
| Onboarding | First week | Service standards, brand values, guest journey, complaint handling |
| Role-specific | Ongoing | Skills for specific position (check-in procedures, F&B service, housekeeping standards) |
| Service recovery | Quarterly | Role-play scenarios, LEARN framework practice |
| Guest feedback review | Monthly | Share top themes from reviews; celebrate wins, address patterns |
| Standards refresher | Bi-annually | Full standards review and recommitment |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Written standards not trained | Staff don't know what's expected | Standards must be trained, not just handed out |
| Complaint escalated but not followed up | Guest doesn't feel resolution was completed | Follow up with every guest who complained within 24 hours |
| Negative reviews unanswered | Future guests see unresolved complaints | Reply to 100% of reviews, positive and negative |
| Guest preferences not recorded in PMS | Repeat guests treated like first-timers | Build preference capture into check-in and record in PMS |
| Service recovery compensation has no guidelines | Inconsistent: under- or over-compensating | Create a compensation matrix; train and empower front desk |

## Quick Diagnostic

| Question | If No | Action |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Are written service standards in place for every guest touchpoint? | Inconsistent experience | Write and train the standards |
| Do all staff know the LEARN service recovery framework? | Complaints handled inconsistently | Conduct a LEARN training session this week |
| Are all online reviews responded to within 48 hours? | Reviews going unanswered | Assign a review response owner with a daily SLA |
| Are guest preferences being captured and actioned? | Repeat guests treated as new guests | Audit the PMS preference fields; brief front desk |
