# Site Inspection Skill

A structured framework for conducting construction site inspections that catch quality issues early, verify compliance, and create the documentation trail needed for client confidence and regulatory compliance. Great site inspection is systematic, evidence-based, and drives corrective action — not just observation.

## Core Principle

**Inspect early, inspect often, document everything.** The cost of rectifying a defect increases exponentially the later it is found. A defect found before concrete is poured costs minutes. The same defect found after handover costs weeks. Systematic inspection is the most cost-effective quality tool on any construction project.

## Scoring

**Goal: 10/10.** When reviewing site inspection practices, rate 0–10 based on inspection frequency, use of structured checklists, quality of documentation, speed of corrective action, and hold point compliance. A 10/10 means all hold points are inspected before work proceeds, defects are documented with photos and closed within agreed timeframes, and the inspection register is current and audit-ready.

## Inspection Framework

### 1. Types of Inspection

**Core concept:** Different inspections serve different purposes and require different preparation.

| Inspection Type | Purpose | When | Who |
|----------------|---------|------|-----|
| **Daily site walk** | Identify immediate safety hazards and obvious defects | Daily (start of shift) | Site manager |
| **Weekly quality inspection** | Systematic review of all active work areas | Weekly | PM or site manager + subcontractor foreman |
| **Hold point inspection** | Verify specific critical activity before work proceeds | Per ITP / programme | PM / certifier (as required) |
| **Witness point inspection** | Observe critical activity as it occurs | Per ITP | PM / certifier (as required) |
| **Pre-concrete pour inspection** | Verify reinforcement, formwork, and services before pour | Before every pour | PM + certifier |
| **Pre-cover inspection** | Verify work before it is covered (framing, rough-in services) | Before lining/cladding | PM |
| **Practical completion inspection** | Final defect identification before handover | End of project | PM + client |
| **Regulatory inspection** | Compliance verification by certifier or authority | Per regulatory schedule | Private certifier / council inspector |

### 2. Inspection & Test Plan (ITP)

**Core concept:** A document that lists every inspection required on the project, the standard it must meet, and who performs and witnesses the inspection.

**Why it matters:** Without an ITP, critical inspections are missed. The ITP is both a quality management tool and a contractual and regulatory requirement on most projects.

**ITP columns:**

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Activity | Work activity being inspected |
| Reference | Specification clause, drawing number, or standard |
| Inspection type | H (Hold), W (Witness), R (Review records), S (Surveillance) |
| Frequency | How often the inspection occurs |
| Responsibility | Who performs the inspection (contractor, certifier, authority) |
| Record | What documentation is required (checklist, test report, photo) |

**Hold point vs. Witness point:**
- **Hold point (H):** Work CANNOT proceed until the inspection has been performed and signed off. A missed hold point is a non-conformance.
- **Witness point (W):** The inspector is notified and given the opportunity to attend. Work may proceed if the inspector does not attend within the agreed notification period.

See: [references/itp-template.md](references/itp-template.md)

### 3. Conducting the Inspection

**Core concept:** A systematic, evidence-based inspection that produces a documented record.

**Pre-inspection preparation:**
- Bring the relevant inspection checklist for the activity
- Bring the relevant drawings and specifications
- Review the previous inspection report — are prior defects closed?
- Notify the relevant subcontractor foreman to accompany you

**During the inspection:**
- Work through the checklist systematically — do not freehand it
- Document findings with photos (date/time-stamped)
- Note exact location of any defects (grid reference, floor, room number)
- Rate defect severity (see section 4)
- Discuss findings with the subcontractor foreman on site
- Obtain signature from subcontractor acknowledging defects found

**After the inspection:**
- Upload photos and complete the inspection report same day
- Issue a Defect Notice or NCR (Non-Conformance Report) for all defects found
- Update the defect register
- Confirm corrective action timeline with the subcontractor
- Schedule re-inspection

### 4. Defect Classification & Management

**Core concept:** A consistent system for classifying, recording, and tracking all defects to closure.

| Category | Definition | Required Action |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Critical** | Safety hazard or structural defect; work must stop | Stop work; immediate rectification; NCR issued; notify PM and client |
| **Major** | Defect that affects function, performance, or compliance | Rectify before work proceeds to next phase; NCR issued |
| **Minor** | Cosmetic defect that does not affect function | Add to defect register; rectify before practical completion |

**Defect register fields:**
- Defect ID (sequential)
- Date identified
- Location (area, floor, grid)
- Description
- Category (Critical / Major / Minor)
- Subcontractor responsible
- Photo reference
- Corrective action required
- Due date for rectification
- Date closed
- Closed by

### 5. Non-Conformance Reports (NCR)

**Core concept:** A formal record that work has not met the specified requirements — and a controlled process to resolve it.

**Why it matters:** NCRs create accountability, prevent recurrence, and protect the principal contractor if the subcontractor disputes the issue at final account.

**When to issue an NCR:**
- Work does not meet the specification or drawing
- A hold point was not called (work proceeded without inspection)
- Material delivered to site does not meet specification
- Test results are outside acceptance criteria

**NCR content:**
1. Description of non-conformance (what was found, where, when)
2. Reference to the specification clause or drawing being breached
3. Proposed corrective action
4. Proposed preventive action (how to stop recurrence)
5. Signoff by subcontractor
6. Signoff by PM when corrective action is verified complete

### 6. Practical Completion Inspection

**Core concept:** A systematic walkthrough of the entire project with the client to identify all outstanding defects before issuing a practical completion certificate.

**Why it matters:** Practical completion triggers the defects liability period clock, triggers the bulk of payment, and transfers risk to the client for the works. It must be done rigorously.

**PC inspection process:**
1. Complete all works and internal defect clearing before inviting the client
2. Conduct a joint walkthrough with the client and document all defects on a formal defect schedule
3. Agree a timeframe for rectification of each defect
4. Issue the practical completion certificate once agreement is reached
5. Rectify all defects within the agreed period
6. Final inspection to verify all defects closed

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Inspecting without a checklist | Inconsistent; things get missed | Mandatory checklist for every inspection type |
| No photos to document defects | Word-of-mouth disputes; no evidence | Minimum 2 photos per defect (overview + close-up) |
| Defects raised but never tracked to closure | Defects reappear at PC or post-handover | Weekly defect register review; escalate overdue items |
| Hold point missed (work proceeded without inspection) | NCR; potential rework or regulatory issue | Brief all subcontractor foremen on hold points at project start |
| PC inspection done without client | Client finds defects post-handover; disputes | Always conduct PC inspection jointly with the client |

## Quick Diagnostic

| Question | If No | Action |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Is there an ITP in place and being followed? | Hold points may be missed | Develop ITP; brief subcontractors on hold points |
| Is the defect register current (updated after every inspection)? | Defects falling through the cracks | Update defect register same day as inspection |
| Are NCRs being issued for non-conformances? | No accountability for subcontractors | Issue NCR for every non-conformance; track to closure |
| Are pre-cover inspections being called before linings are installed? | Hidden defects under linings | Make pre-cover inspection a mandatory hold point |
