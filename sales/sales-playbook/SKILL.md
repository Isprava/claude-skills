# Sales Playbook Skill

A structured, repeatable sales process that guides every deal from first contact to closed-won. Great sales processes eliminate guesswork, create consistent customer experiences, and make individual reps more effective — so success is replicable, not accidental.

## Core Principle

**Sell by helping, not by pitching.** The best salespeople are skilled at understanding problems deeply before proposing solutions. Prospects buy from people who make them feel understood — not from people who talk the most about their product.

## Scoring

**Goal: 10/10.** When reviewing a sales process, deal, or call, rate 0–10 based on qualification rigor, customer understanding, solution fit, and deal momentum. A 10/10 means the rep fully understands the customer's problem, the deal is qualified, next steps are clear, and the proposal matches the need.

## Sales Process

### Stage 1: Prospecting & Outreach

**Core concept:** Identify and reach out to prospects who match your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).

**Why it matters:** Time spent on the wrong prospects is the #1 productivity killer in sales.

**ICP definition checklist:**
- Company size (employees, revenue)
- Industry / vertical
- Geography
- Trigger events (new funding, leadership change, regulation change, expansion)
- Pain profile — what problem do they have that you uniquely solve?

**Outreach best practices:**
- Personalize every outreach — reference something specific about the company or person
- Lead with their problem, not your product
- Short emails win: 3–5 sentences maximum for cold outreach
- Follow up 5–7 times across multiple channels (email, LinkedIn, phone)
- Stop after 7 touches with no response — move on

See: [references/outreach-templates.md](references/outreach-templates.md)

### Stage 2: Discovery

**Core concept:** A deep conversation to understand the prospect's situation, problem, goals, and decision process — before any solution is proposed.

**Why it matters:** Discovery quality determines close rate. Reps who skip discovery pitch to people who aren't ready to buy.

**Discovery framework (SPIN):**

| Question Type | Purpose | Example |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| **Situation** | Understand current state | "Walk me through how you currently manage your construction scheduling." |
| **Problem** | Uncover pain points | "Where does that process break down for you?" |
| **Implication** | Explore impact of the problem | "What happens to a project when scheduling slips?" |
| **Need-Payoff** | Surface value of solving it | "What would it mean for your business if you could eliminate scheduling delays?" |

**Discovery call structure:**
1. Set agenda (2 min)
2. Ask situation questions (10 min)
3. Ask problem and implication questions (15 min)
4. Explore their goals and success criteria (10 min)
5. Understand decision process (5 min): Who decides? What's the timeline? What's the budget?
6. Summarize what you heard and propose a next step (5 min)

### Stage 3: Qualification

**Core concept:** Confirm the deal is worth pursuing before investing significant time in proposal or demo.

**Why it matters:** Unqualified deals waste everyone's time and inflate pipeline metrics dishonestly.

**Qualification framework (MEDDIC):**

| Letter | Field | Key Question |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| M | Metrics | What measurable outcome does the customer need? |
| E | Economic Buyer | Who controls the budget and signs the contract? |
| D | Decision Criteria | What factors matter most in their decision? |
| D | Decision Process | What steps do they follow to make a purchase decision? |
| I | Identify Pain | What is the specific pain that creates urgency? |
| C | Champion | Who inside the organization wants this to succeed? |

**Disqualification signals — walk away if:**
- No clear budget or budget is far below your minimum deal size
- No timeline ("we're just exploring")
- No access to the economic buyer
- No identified pain or urgency

### Stage 4: Solution & Demo

**Core concept:** Present a tailored solution that directly addresses the specific problems uncovered in discovery.

**Why it matters:** Generic demos lose deals. Tailored demos win them.

**Demo best practices:**
- Open by recapping the customer's stated problems (shows you listened)
- Structure the demo around their use cases, not your feature list
- Show the outcome first, then the feature that delivers it
- Invite questions throughout — don't monologue
- End with: "Based on what you've seen, does this solve the problem you described?"

### Stage 5: Proposal & Negotiation

**Core concept:** A clear, specific proposal tied to the value you will deliver — followed by confident negotiation.

**Why it matters:** A vague proposal creates uncertainty; uncertainty kills deals.

**Proposal must-haves:**
- Restate the customer's problem in their own words
- Show the proposed solution and how it maps to their problem
- Quantify the expected value (time saved, cost avoided, revenue gained)
- Pricing — clear and confident, not apologetic
- Timeline and next steps

**Negotiation principles:**
- Anchor high — first offer should be your ideal outcome
- Never discount without getting something in return ("I can offer X if you can commit to signing by end of month")
- Know your BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement) before the call
- Silence is a negotiation tool — let the other side fill it

See: [references/objection-handling.md](references/objection-handling.md)

### Stage 6: Close & Handoff

**Core concept:** Ask for the business clearly and make the transition to delivery seamless.

**Why it matters:** Many deals are lost not due to lack of interest but because nobody asked clearly.

**Closing techniques:**
- **Assumptive close**: "What's the best way to get the contract to you?"
- **Summary close**: Recap value → "Based on everything we've discussed, are you ready to move forward?"
- **Timeline close**: "To meet your [deadline], we'd need to start by [date]. Can we commit to that?"

**Handoff checklist:**
- [ ] Customer expectations documented (scope, timeline, deliverables)
- [ ] Customer success / delivery team briefed
- [ ] Kickoff scheduled
- [ ] CRM updated with close date, deal value, and key contacts

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Skipping discovery to pitch faster | Pitching the wrong solution → lost deal | Never demo or propose before completing discovery |
| Unqualified pipeline | False confidence, wasted proposal time | Apply MEDDIC at stage 3; remove unqualified deals |
| Generic demo | Prospect doesn't see their problem solved | Open every demo by replaying their stated problems |
| Discounting without reciprocity | Trains customers to ask for discounts every time | Always get something in return for any discount |
| No clear next step at end of every meeting | Deal loses momentum and stalls | End every meeting with a scheduled, agreed next step |

## Quick Diagnostic

| Question | If No | Action |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Is the Economic Buyer engaged? | Risk of losing to internal politics | Get a meeting with the EB before investing in proposal |
| Is there a defined timeline from the customer? | Deal may stall indefinitely | Ask directly: "When do you need this in place?" |
| Do you have a named champion inside the company? | No internal advocate | Develop a champion or disqualify |
| Is the next step scheduled on the calendar? | Deal will stall | Do not end any meeting without scheduling the next one |
